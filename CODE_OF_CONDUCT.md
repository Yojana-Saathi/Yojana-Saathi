# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

YojanaSaathi is an open source project for public-interest technology. We are committed to a respectful, inclusive, and productive community for contributors of all backgrounds and experience levels.

We expect everyone who participates in this project to act with care, professionalism, and good faith.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that helps the community:

- Use welcoming and respectful language.
- Assume good intent, especially when reviewing work from new contributors.
- Give specific, actionable feedback.
- Respect different viewpoints and lived experiences.
- Keep discussion focused on the project, its users, and the quality of the work.
- Acknowledge mistakes and correct them quickly.
- Be careful with topics involving identity, public services, citizen data, and eligibility decisions.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or sustained disruption.
- Insults, personal attacks, or demeaning comments.
- Discriminatory language or behavior.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted attention.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Dismissing security, privacy, or accessibility concerns without review.
- Using project spaces to spam, advertise unrelated products, or derail work.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, commits, code review, community calls, and any public or private communication made on behalf of the project.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take action when behavior harms the project or community. Actions may include:

- A private clarification or reminder.
- A formal warning.
- Temporary limits on participation.
- Removal from a discussion, issue, pull request, or project space.
- A permanent ban from project participation.

Enforcement decisions should be proportionate, documented where appropriate, and focused on protecting contributors and users.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, contact a project maintainer privately. Include:

- What happened.
- Where it happened.
- Who was involved.
- Any relevant links, screenshots, or timestamps.

Maintainers will review reports with discretion and will avoid sharing reporter details beyond what is needed to resolve the issue.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for setting the tone of the project. They should:

- Model respectful technical disagreement.
- Review contributions fairly.
- Protect contributors from harassment or bad-faith participation.
- Treat security, privacy, accessibility, and user-safety concerns seriously.
- Apply this Code of Conduct consistently.

## Attribution

This policy is inspired by widely used open source community standards and adapted for YojanaSaathi's public-interest, citizen-data context.
